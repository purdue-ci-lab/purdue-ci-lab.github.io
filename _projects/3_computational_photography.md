Computational photography treats the camera and the algorithm as a single, jointly designed system. By co-optimizing optics, sensing, and reconstruction, we can capture information that no individual component could recover on its own.

We are particularly interested in neural-field representations that compactly model scenes, light transport, and appearance, and in how they can be supervised from unconventional measurements.
