Passive single-photon cameras can record a scene without any controlled light source, capturing photon arrivals across an enormous range of timescales. From a single passive capture, the same data can be re-rendered as video spanning many orders of magnitude in time.

This line of work explores the sensing models, reconstruction algorithms, and applications that ultra-wideband passive imaging makes possible.
